Company Hiring Insights

Google

Google's hiring process is known for being rigorous yet rewarding, focusing on GCA (General Cognitive Ability), Role-Related Knowledge (RRK), Leadership, and Googliness.

Step 1: Application & Online Assessment

Review of resume followed by an online coding challenge for technical roles.

Step 2: Phone Interviews

1-2 technical phone screens focusing on DSA and basic problem solving.

Step 3: Onsite Interviews

4-5 rounds covering coding, system design, and behavioral questions.

Step 4: Hiring Committee Review

Independent committee reviews interview feedback and makes the final call.

Amazon

Amazon heavily emphasizes its 16 Leadership Principles throughout the interview process, alongside strong technical skills.

Step 1: Online Assessment (OA)

Coding problems and a simulation of typical work scenarios at Amazon.

Step 2: "The Loop" (Onsite)

A series of interviews where each interviewer focuses on specific Leadership Principles.

Step 3: Bar Raiser

A special round with a seasoned interviewer who ensures every hire raises the company's average performance.

Microsoft

Microsoft looks for "Learn-it-all" individuals who show passion for technology and collaboration.

Step 1: Screening

Recruiter call or short technical screening on fundamental concepts.

Step 2: Technical Rounds

Deep dive into data structures, algorithms, and often code quality/readability.

Step 3: AA Interview (As Appropriate)

A final round with a senior leader to assess culture fit and potential.
